2018 Suzuki Jimny

Let us introduce to you the all new Suzuki Jimny, completely redesinged from bottom up for 2018. It may look a bit like a miniaturized Mercedes G-Class but a Mercedes Benz it is not. Unpretentious and built for the masses who desire small, affordable, functional daily driver yet very capable off-roader. The boxiness of this vehicle might put off others but it creates roomier interior for it's size. With a capacity to hold four adults, the passengers in the back might be wanting more leg room on extended drives. What it lacks in roominiess, the Jimny makes up in the fun (precisely because of its size and agility), affordabitly and practicality department. Don't let its size fool you, this vehicle is in par if not better in pretty much every aspect than it's larger SUV competitors which are four to eight times more expensive.

Being tiny has many benefits. For one having a small footprint will allow you to take this vehicle in the most congested parts of the city and/or woods where many regular size vehicles will simply not fit. City driving for the Jimny is a breeze with its tight turning radius, able to drive through narrow spaces and park in extra small parking spaces which larger vehicle dare not go due to fear of damaging their vehiche in the process of parking. Tiny and light weight go hand-in-hand, it's no surprise that this vehicle gets excellent gas mileage. Reliablity, cost of ownership along with maintenance through the life of the vehicle rank high when compared to its bigger and more expensive competitors.

The new 3-door Jimny is still true to its herritage. Underneath is still based around a traditional steel, ladder frame chassis designed with greater strengh and stiffness to support the front and rear axels with separate differentials. Powered by a 1.5 liter four-cylinder petrol engine with 101bhp and 96lb ft. This is 24 more HP and 200cc greater in capacity than its predecessor.

Heritage

Despite only having four iteration with the latest Jimny, its DNA can be traced back when the first model was showcased in 1970. Since then it has gained greater admirations and love from customers with every new generation. Earning it a reputation as small, lightweight authentic off-roader and is a masterpiece of Suzuki 4WD.

1st Generation

The first generation Jimny made its debut as the only compact 4WD in the minicar segment. This is what started it all. With its small size but powerful performance, it earned high acclaim not only from professional drivers but from its owners as well. Its affordability only added to its popularity.

2nd Generation

The second generation gained greater accolades as it became an unprecedented global hit. Redesigned with a more solid square shape which created roomier interior. It became popular with both genders as its sharp, stylish design suited urban and off-road settings.

3rd Generation

The third iteration introduced a higher level of comfort with its aerodynamic body and a fixed hard roof for its passengers. No longer an open air cabin with a slight increase in overall dimensions including increased wheelbase and refined ridgid suspension, the Jimny offered greater ride comfort, improved stability and manuverability both on and off-road. The longest iteration without redesigned, it cemented its place among best 4WD vehicle and stayed popular for 20 years.

Interior Features

Focused on Driving Experience

The cabin is simple yet comfortable providing its driver and occupants practicality. Black interior throughout including cloth seats, side panels and dashboard. The gauge cluster is large and cleverly laid out with buttons and knobs designed for easy reach and operation. Nothing in the interior distracts the driver from serious driving whether navigating through the urban jungle or exploring the off beaten path.

Photo

Sensible Design

Much can be said in the simple, straight forward and sensible design of the interior from the minimal color scheme to the button and knobs that can be operated even with gloves. This is the epitomy of utalitarianism in a vehicle without taking away all the creature comforts of a modern vehicle.

Speaking of modern conveniences, the Jimny offers seven-inch infotainment system, as well as steering-mounted buttons, automatic climate control, cruise control and more importantly an autonomous emergency braking system.

Photo

Practical Storage

The vehicle offers adequate storage around the cabin for its passengers. There are two cup holders on the center console with a small container next to the handbrake, a glove compartment on passenger side and door pocket on both doors. The rear seats splip 50/50 or both can be folded completely flat for maximum cargo capacity when needed.

Performance

Ridgid Ladder Frame

The new Jimny is based around an all-new, rugged ladder-frame chassis, designed to protect the Jimny's reputation as a mountain goat, capable of going places other SUVs fear to tread.

Photo

3-Link Ridgid Axle Suspension with Coil Springs

Another feature that makes this vehicle a legitimate off-roader is it's full-width rigid axles in both the front and rear. When an obstacle pushes one wheel up, the axle presses the other wheel down to increase tire contact in rough road conditions proving you grip at all times.

Photo

4WD with Low Range Transfer Gear

On flat surface off-roads shift the available transfer lever 4H to enjoy 4WD traction and 2H (2WD) on paved roads for a smoother, quieter ride. When the going gets tough shift to 4L for maximum torque and traction.

Photo

1.5 Liter Engine

The engine has also been upgraded from the outgoing 1.3L engine to a new 1.5L which generates strong torque throughout a wide rpm range for powerful off-road performance. Small and lightweight, it also delivers high fuel efficiency.

Ample Ground Clearance and Body Angles

Handle any outdoor adventures with an ample departure angle (49°), ramp breakover angle (28°), approach angle (37°) and ground clearance (210mm).

Photo

Brake LSD Traction Control

If at any time two wheels diagonal from each other lose grip while you’re travelling over slippery surfaces, the system will automatically brake the slipping wheels and redistribute torque to the other two wheels, allowing the Jimny to make its escape.

Photo

Hill Hold Control

Move upward and onward when starting on steep slopes. Even on bumpy slopes that vary on the right and left sides, this feature helps prevent rearward rolling so you can focus on controlling acceleration.

Photo

Hill Descent Control

On tricky descents, with a push of a button, the system is designed to automatically apply the brakes and maintain a fixed speed so you can concentrate on steering without using the brake pedal or clutch.

Safety

DSBS

1. Alerts the driver with an audio warning and visual warning.

2. Deploys brake assist to increase braking force if the risk of collision is high and the driver panic brakes.

3. Applies strong automatic braking if the risk of collision increases even more.

After a long day, it helps to have some support. If the vehicle is “wandering” due to your drowsiness or fatigue, this system is designed to issue audio and visual warnings to keep you alert.

The Jimny helps you stay on track. Should you unintentionally stray from your lane while driving at high speed, this feature is designed to vibrate the steering wheel and issue a visual warning to encourage safer driving.
